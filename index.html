Ongoing Doctoral Research 2 +−
Organizing Humanitarian Aid in South Sudan
with IMPACT Initiatives. Supported by ReCIPE CEPR and SurveyCTO
How should we invest in and allocate humanitarian aid in conflict-affected economies? I combine six years of geospatial data on trade, aid, and conflict across South Sudanese marketplaces to study the spatial allocation of humanitarian aid.
In Progress +
Informality on the Intensive Margin in Zambia
Supported by IGC, UNU-WIDER, STEG-CEPR
Do formal firms operate informally, and why? I study the rollout of electronic invoicing in Zambia during a period of daily power shortages to understand how firms navigate formality when public infrastructure fails.

Policy Contributions 2 +−
E-invoicing adoption under loadshedding in Zambia
with S. Jackson — IGC Policy Brief, 2025
IGC Brief ↗
Since 2024, all Zambian firms must use electronic invoices for VAT reporting. Based on interviews with 61 firms, we study how daily loadshedding disrupts adoption: firms unable to issue smart invoices lose formal customers and revert to informal trade, even as high awareness and compliance efforts by the Zambia Revenue Authority promise long-term gains in tax revenue.

About

I'm a third-year PhD candidate in Economics at the Geneva Graduate Institute, supervised by Pr. Cajal-Grossi and Pr. Raisaro. My research interests are in development economics and trade.

Before the PhD, I worked as a Trade Information Specialist at the International Trade Centre (UNCTAD/WTO) and as a Research Assistant at the World Trade Organization. I hold an MSc in Economics from the Geneva Graduate Institute and a BSc in Economics from HEC Lausanne.

I speak French, Italian, English, and Portuguese.
